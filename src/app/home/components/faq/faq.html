What is a secured transaction? +

A secured transaction is a transaction between a lender and a borrower where the borrower gives the lender a security interest in collateral (movable property) to secure the loan. This allows the lender to repossess the collateral if the borrower defaults.

Who can use the registry? +

The registry is primarily for lenders (Financial Service Providers), but the public can use it for public searches. Only registered users, such as Financial Service Providers, can perform full registrations and transactions.

How do I register collateral? +

To register collateral, you must be a registered user (e.g., a Financial Service Provider). You'll log in to the system, navigate to the registration section, and enter the required details about the collateral and the security agreement.

What happens after discharge? +

After a security interest is discharged, the record is updated in the registry to reflect that the debt has been fully paid or the agreement has been terminated. The security interest is no longer active, and the collateral is no longer tied to that specific transaction.

What are movable assets? +

Movable assets are things that can be moved from one place to another, such as vehicles, machinery, or livestock.

What can be considered movable? +

Examples include vehicles, equipment, inventory, livestock, or other tangible assets that can be relocated.

What are immovable assets? +

Immovable assets are things that cannot be moved, such as land, buildings, or permanent structures.

What can be considered immovable? +

Examples include houses, commercial buildings, plots of land, and other fixed structures.
