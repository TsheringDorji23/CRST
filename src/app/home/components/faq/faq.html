Frequently Asked Questions

General FAQs

What is iCoMS? +

iCoMS is a comprehensive, national digital platform developed to streamline the registration, management, and public disclosure of security interests in both movable and immovable property.

Who operates iCoMS? +

iCoMS is implemented through institutional collaboration between the Royal Monetary Authority of Bhutan (RMA) and the National Land Commission Secretariat (NLCS).

Who can have access to the iCoMS? +

For FSPs: iCoMS will provide access to FSPs with user identifications for registration, amendment, continuation, discharge and release purpose.

For Public: iCoMS will give access to the general public to conduct search services to discover active registrations made on the collateral.

What is the Asset Identification Number in Movable Collaterals? +

Asset Identification Numbers are of two types and are classified as below:

a. Serial-Based Numbers:

These numbers are assigned to all vehicle types. Each vehicle is uniquely identified by its serial-based number.

b. General-Based Numbers:

These numbers are used for movable assets other than vehicles. Examples include:

  • Insurance Policy Number
  • Work Order Number
  • Shares Reference Number
How do I amend a registration? +

All amendments are carried out by amending the initial Registration Statement electronically through the iCoMS system using the same registration Number.

What are the processes for registrations? +

Registrations establish public notice and priority. For movable property, details such as the debtor’s identification number, a description of the collateral (which can be specific or generic), the secured party's information, and the amount secured need to be provided. For immovable property, registration requires the debtor's identification number and a precise description of the land using the Thram and Plot numbers.

When can iCoMS be accessed? +

Electronic access is generally available 24/7, except during scheduled maintenance periods.

How does fraud prevention work in the registry system? +

Fraud prevention in iCoMS works by preventing double registration of the same asset during mortgaging, avoiding duplicate insurance claims for insured vehicles, and relying on verified user accounts, standardized identification criteria, audit trails, and public searchability to reduce fraudulent activities

Who can conduct searches and how? +

Any person may conduct a search by providing the details required for search. It is the only service available for the general public. For FSPs, conducting a search is mandatory before registering any security interest. For ease of conducting search and to avoid confusion the details requirements are put into dropdown lists. With a minimum fee charged through the payment gateway, the report will be mailed in the provided mail address.

How do I make payment during search? +

Payment during search is made through the iCoMS integrated online payment gateway; after completing and submitting all required search details, the payment option will appear, allowing the user to pay the minimal search fee and obtain the Certified Search Report.

What is Movable Property? +

“Movable property” means documents of title, goods, instruments, intangibles, money and securities and includes fixtures and addition but does not include building materials that have been affixed to immovable property.

What constitutes Immovable Property? +

Immovable property primarily includes land and any permanent structures or interests attached to it, such as buildings, flats, etc…

What can I register under iCoMS? +

Financial Service Providers (FSPs) can register both movable and immovable property for borrowers who wish to access financial services by pledging their assets as security.

a. Movable Collateral Assets:

FSPs can register pledged movable assets as collateral, including:

  • Vehicles (all types)
  • Accounts receivable
  • Shares and securities
  • Plant and machinery
  • Stock and inventories
  • Project assets (including multiple movable items)
  • Others

b. Immovable Property:

FSPs can register mortgaged immovable properties as collateral, including:

  • Land
  • Buildings
  • Flats
